Join BAHE

Opportunities for postdoctoral fellows, PhD and MRes students, visiting researchers and collaborators interested in biodiversity, wildlife ecology, One Health, ecological interactions and quantitative ecology.

Join BAHE

BAHE welcomes motivated researchers at different career stages who are driven by scientific curiosity, committed to rigorous ecological research and interested in understanding biodiversity, wildlife health, ecological interactions and socioecological systems in a rapidly changing world.

We particularly encourage individuals who enjoy integrating field observation, molecular and biodiversity data, conceptual thinking and quantitative analysis to address important ecological questions while contributing to biodiversity conservation, ecosystem resilience and sustainable futures.

Research environment

Joining BAHE means becoming part of a collaborative, research-led environment where ecological questions drive methodological development and where field observation, biological understanding and quantitative analysis are integrated throughout the research process.

We aim to foster intellectual curiosity, scientific integrity and analytical rigour while encouraging researchers to think across disciplines and scales—from organisms and ecological interactions to ecosystems, environmental change and human wellbeing.

Researchers are encouraged to develop independence, collaborate openly and contribute to an evolving programme of research that advances both ecological understanding and evidence-based decision making.

Postdoctoral fellowships

Rather than recruiting only through advertised positions, BAHE welcomes discussions with outstanding early-career researchers interested in developing competitive fellowship applications.

We are particularly interested in researchers whose ideas align with BAHE’s interests in biodiversity, wildlife ecology, ecological interactions, One Health, quantitative ecology and sustainable futures.

Potential fellowship schemes include:

  • Royal Society University Research Fellowships
  • Royal Society Dorothy Hodgkin Fellowships
  • Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships
  • UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) fellowships
  • Leverhulme Early Career Fellowships
  • NERC Independent Research Fellowships
  • Wellcome Trust fellowships
  • Other appropriate national and international fellowship schemes

Prospective applicants are encouraged to contact us well in advance of application deadlines to discuss scientific fit, proposal development and institutional support.

PhD opportunities

BAHE welcomes prospective doctoral researchers who are excited by fundamental ecological questions and motivated to contribute to rigorous, interdisciplinary science.

Projects are normally developed collaboratively around shared scientific interests, available expertise and suitable funding opportunities rather than from predefined project lists.

Research interests currently include: - biodiversity responses to environmental change - wildlife ecology, parasites and microbiomes - ecological interactions and community assembly - One Health and disease ecology - ecological modelling and Bayesian statistics - biodiversity monitoring and evidence synthesis - restoration ecology and ecosystem resilience - nature, wellbeing and sustainable futures

Strong applicants typically demonstrate: - genuine scientific curiosity - excellent academic preparation - independent thinking and initiative - strong communication skills - quantitative, field or laboratory experience appropriate to their background - enthusiasm for interdisciplinary ecological research

MRes research

The MRes provides intensive research experience within an active research environment and is an excellent pathway towards doctoral research, conservation science, quantitative ecology or related research careers.

Potential research areas include:

  • wildlife ecology and disease ecology
  • biodiversity monitoring and ecological modelling
  • urban ecology and One Health
  • tropical ecology and conservation
  • ecological interactions and ecological networks
  • microbiomes and host-associated communities
  • evidence synthesis and meta-analysis

Applicants commonly come from backgrounds including ecology, zoology, biology, environmental science, geography, conservation, veterinary biosciences, mathematics, statistics or data science.

For information about entry requirements, programme structure and university regulations, please see the Swansea University Biosciences MRes programme.

Visiting researchers and students

Subject to available capacity, BAHE welcomes enquiries from:

  • visiting PhD students
  • visiting postdoctoral researchers
  • undergraduate and postgraduate research placements
  • research interns
  • international visiting researchers
  • academic visitors interested in collaborative research

Visits are normally developed around shared research interests and ongoing projects.

Research culture

Research philosophy: Curiosity about the diversity, complexity and resilience of the natural world is the starting point of scientific discovery and the motivation for understanding ecological change. BAHE integrates field observation, conceptual thinking, quantitative analysis and interdisciplinary synthesis to reveal how interactions among organisms, ecosystems and people shape biodiversity across scales. We aim to develop independent scientists who combine intellectual curiosity with analytical rigour, advancing ecological understanding while generating evidence that supports biodiversity conservation, ecosystem resilience and informed decisions for sustainable futures.

Research environment and professional development

Field ecology

Ecological observation, biodiversity monitoring, wildlife sampling, study design and long-term ecological research.

Quantitative ecology

R programming, Bayesian statistics, ecological modelling, simulation, reproducible workflows and ecological inference.

Scientific thinking

Conceptual development, hypothesis formulation, critical interpretation, evidence synthesis and interdisciplinary integration.

Scientific communication

Scientific writing, visualisation, presentations, collaborative research, open science and knowledge exchange.

Conservation science

Connecting ecological understanding with biodiversity conservation, restoration, wildlife health and sustainable futures.

Research independence

Developing original ideas, collaborative networks, grant writing and long-term scientific leadership.

What we look for

Beyond academic achievement, we value researchers who demonstrate:

  • curiosity about the natural world
  • enthusiasm for ecological discovery
  • commitment to scientific excellence
  • integrity and openness in research
  • willingness to learn across disciplines
  • collaborative spirit
  • critical thinking and intellectual independence
  • motivation to contribute knowledge that benefits biodiversity and society

How to get in touch

If you are interested in joining BAHE, please send a concise email including:

  1. the type of opportunity you are interested in (Postdoctoral Fellowship, PhD, MRes, visiting researcher or placement);
  2. a brief description of your research interests;
  3. how your interests align with BAHE research;
  4. relevant experience and technical skills;
  5. information on existing or potential funding (where applicable);
  6. a short curriculum vitae.

Strong enquiries usually demonstrate scientific curiosity, familiarity with relevant literature and a clear explanation of why BAHE provides an appropriate environment for developing the proposed research. We do not expect a complete research proposal, but we encourage applicants to identify one or two ecological questions they find particularly exciting and explain how they would like to explore them.

Equality, diversity and inclusion

BAHE seeks to build a research community in which curiosity, scientific integrity and mutual respect provide the foundation for collaboration and discovery. We believe that excellent science is strengthened by diversity in people, disciplines, experiences, cultures and ideas, and that tackling complex ecological challenges requires openness to different perspectives and ways of thinking. We welcome talented researchers from all backgrounds who share a commitment to rigorous, collaborative and inclusive research.
