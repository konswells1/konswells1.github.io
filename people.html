People

A collaborative and internationally connected research group

BAHE brings together a research community of staff, postgraduate researchers, MRes students, visiting students, collaborators and alumni working across wildlife ecology, biodiversity science, One Health, ecological modelling and sustainable futures. Our group welcomes people from all backgrounds who are driven by curiosity, enthusiasm for science, and a commitment to sustainability. We value diversity, collaboration, open science and scientific rigour. We strive to create a supportive environment where all members can grow, contribute, and thrive.

Alessandra Giacomini

Postdoctoral Research Associate · Swansea University · Biosciences · She/Her
PhD researcher investigating parasite transmission and relationships between microbiomes, environmental stressors and host health in domesticated and synanthropic species.
Microbiomes Nemabiomes Host Parasite Interactions Parasite Transmission Molecular Ecology Disease Ecology Veterinary Science

Alessandra Giacomini is a Postdoctoral Research Associate with a background in veterinary science whose work investigates the relationships among parasites, microbiomes, environmental stressors and host health.

Her doctoral research examines microbiome community dynamics and sharing among native and invasive tropical small mammals in Borneo. She also investigates host-parasite-microbiome interactions in feral and domesticated horses in temperate grasslands.

Before beginning her PhD, Alessandra completed master's research at the University of Bologna on feline oral squamous cell carcinoma and at the National Veterinary School of Toulouse and CIRAD on the risk of disease transmission through livestock-trading networks.

Her research contributes to BAHE's work on disease ecology, molecular ecology, One Health and the ecological consequences of interactions among hosts, parasites and microbial communities.

Outside research, Alessandra enjoys cooking, spending time with her dog and playing board games.

Mollie Townsend

PhD Researcher · Swansea University · biosciences · She/Her
PhD researcher investigating vulture movement ecology, species distributions and macroecological responses to global environmental change.
Movement Ecology Animal Movement Vultures Species Distribution Modelling Macroecology GPS Tracking Citizen Science

Mollie Townsend is a movement ecologist whose research investigates how individual animal movements scale up to determine species distributions and biodiversity patterns across landscapes. Her work integrates high-frequency GPS tracking, citizen science observations and macroecological modelling to understand how environmental change shapes the ecology and conservation of wide-ranging species.

Mollie completed both her BSc and MSc at the University of Exeter, where she investigated variation in thermoregulatory behaviour in relation to life-history traits in wild cricket populations. As a PhD researcher at Swansea University, she studies vulture movement ecology, combining tracking data with citizen science records to develop models that link individual behaviour with large-scale patterns of species distribution and range dynamics.

Her research contributes to BAHE's work on biodiversity under global change, ecological interactions and sustainable futures by advancing methods that connect movement ecology, macroecology and conservation science. Through integrating behavioural ecology with quantitative modelling, her work helps improve understanding of how species respond to environmental change and how movement information can inform conservation planning at regional and global scales.

Francisca Powell-Romero

Postdoctoral Research Fellow · csiro · Health and Biosecurity · She/Her
Disease ecologist developing ecological forecasting and mathematical models to understand coinfections, disease dynamics and vector-borne disease management.
Disease Ecology Ecological Forecasting Coinfections Infectious Disease Epidemiology Mathematical Modelling Bayesian Statistics

Francisca Powell-Romero is a disease ecologist whose research develops statistical, computational and mathematical approaches to understand and predict infectious disease dynamics. Her work integrates ecological forecasting, coinfection biology and epidemiological modelling to improve prediction of disease risk and support evidence-based decision making.

She completed her PhD at the University of Queensland in 2025 under the supervision of Nicholas Clark, Ricardo Soares Magalhães, Nicholas Fountain-Jones and Konstans Wells. Her doctoral research explored how multivariate ecological models can improve inference and prediction of disease risk using information from coinfections. Prior to her PhD, she completed degrees in Biomedical Science, Sociology and French and was awarded First Class Honours for research investigating the spatiotemporal modelling of Q fever in Queensland.

Francisca is currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (CSIRO), where she develops mathematical and computational models to optimise mosquito-control strategies. Her work combines ecological modelling with stakeholder engagement, collaborating closely with Indigenous communities to ensure that decision-support tools reflect local priorities and culturally informed management approaches.

Her research contributes to BAHE's work on disease ecology, ecological forecasting, quantitative ecology and One Health by advancing methods for predicting infectious disease dynamics across wildlife, environmental and public health systems.

Jack Jefferson completed his Master by Research at Swansea University, where he investigated how pasture management and parasite burden shape the gastrointestinal microbiomes of grazing lambs.
